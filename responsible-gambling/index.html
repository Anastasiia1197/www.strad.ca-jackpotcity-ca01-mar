Strad – For Safe and Balanced Gambling

Last Updated: March 23, 2026

At Strad, we firmly believe that gambling should remain entertainment first and foremost. Our commitment to responsible gambling is at the core of our values. This page provides you with the resources needed to maintain a healthy relationship with gambling and recognize the warning signs of problematic behavior.

Proven Strategies for Healthy and Enjoyable Gaming

Adopting good habits from the start is essential to enjoying gambling responsibly:

  • Set a clear budget: Determine in advance the amount you can afford to spend and never exceed it.
  • Set time limits: Use timers to avoid prolonged gaming sessions.
  • Consider losses as the cost of entertainment: Never gamble with money meant for essential needs.
  • Balance gambling with other activities: Maintain a social life and varied hobbies.
  • Avoid gambling under the influence: Alcohol or substances can impair your judgment.

As addiction prevention expert Dr. Emily Carter notes: "Responsible gambling starts with a conscious attitude and clearly defined limits before you even begin to play."

Identify Warning Signs Before Problems Arise

Recognizing the early signs of problematic behavior is crucial for taking action quickly:

Warning Sign Description Recommended Action
Increasing Bets Betting larger amounts to achieve the same level of excitement Re-evaluate your limits and consult a professional
Obsessive Thoughts Constantly thinking about gambling, even during other activities Take a break and seek support
Lying About Gambling Hiding the extent of your involvement from loved ones Restore honesty and ask for help
Financial Problems Borrowing money or selling possessions to gamble Use self-exclusion tools immediately
Neglecting Responsibilities Neglecting work, studies, or family for gambling Consult an addiction specialist

Debunking Myths and Misconceptions About Gambling

Several mistaken beliefs can encourage risky behavior:

  1. Myth: "I'm bound to win after a losing streak" - Reality: Each game is independent, and probabilities do not change based on previous outcomes.

  2. Myth: "There are foolproof systems to win" - Reality: No system can guarantee long-term profits in games of chance.

  3. Myth: "I can control the outcome through concentration" - Reality: Casino games are based on chance, not skill or concentration.

  4. Myth: "Online gambling is riskier than in-person" - Reality: Regulated platforms offer the same protections, and often more control tools.

Self-Assessment Tools to Stay in Control of Your Gambling

Regularly ask yourself these questions to evaluate your relationship with gambling:

  • ❓ Have you ever gambled more money than you intended?
  • ❓ Have you ever felt guilty after a gambling session?
  • ❓ Have you ever lied about your gambling habits to family or friends?
  • ❓ Has gambling ever affected your work or studies?
  • ❓ Have you ever borrowed money to gamble or pay gambling debts?

If you answer "yes" to several of these questions, it may be time to reconsider your gambling habits and seek help.

Support Systems for Families Affected by Problem Gambling

The impact of problem gambling often affects the entire family. Here's how to support a loved one:

  • Non-accusatory approach: Express your concern without judgment.
  • Educate yourself: Understand the nature of the gambling problem to better help.
  • Encourage seeking help: Offer to accompany your loved one to a consultation.
  • Protect family finances: Implement measures to secure joint accounts.
  • Take care of yourself: Seek support for yourself from specialized organizations.

Commitment to Minor Protection and Responsible Gambling

Strad is strongly committed to protecting minors:

  1. Strict age verification: Rigorous identification process for all new registrants.

  2. Parental controls: Technical tools to prevent minors from accessing our services.

  3. Educational awareness: Information programs about the risks of gambling for young people.

  4. Proactive reporting: Mechanisms to detect and report access attempts by minors.

We encourage parents to:

  • 🔒 Use parental control software on family devices
  • 💬 Have open discussions about the risks of gambling with their children
  • 👁️ Monitor online activities without invading privacy
  • 📚 Educate themselves on signs of early access to gambling

Our commitment to responsible gambling is ongoing. We constantly work to improve our prevention tools and support behavioral addiction research initiatives.
